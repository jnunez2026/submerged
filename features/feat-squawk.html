Squawkbirds

October 27, 2022 -- 9:43 AM

Spirits are high but those notes are even higher yesterday, 26th of October, 2022, as scholars from 9-Peanuts absolutely mopped us during the Music 3 Practical Test. Answering the call, three 9th Grade sections flaunted their feathers and sang their answers. What exactly is this call? It's a Renaissance-style cover of a pop song to show off the strength of the red underline.

From Bohemian Rhapsody, to This Side of Paradise, then finally, to Dancing Queen, I am getting so much anxiety from listening to them. Although the first two sections had pretty alright performances, the last one is just out of the water from the very start.

Peanuts Treasurer Miggy Pangils showcased their now open secret: the red underline. While other sections figured out their tones and voice types, these singing queens just made two lyric sheets and slapped some red underline on them. Pangils said that this was to signify "when we'd do an echo or something."

Oh yeah, they also had Sarmiento fiddling with music theory, but that's not the main point. That's nerd stuff. What matters is Johana's inhumane notes. If the red underlines weren't enough, then those will shatter your glass eyes and it'll flood boo-boo water. Seriously, how is that possible?

Hence, take those lines, those nerd stuff, and those whistles, and you get myself weak like a soggy tissue getting blown by the wind. Will we ever top this? I have no clue.
