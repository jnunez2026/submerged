Spooky Scary Faculty Stalls

October 28, 2022 -- 12:08 PM

Maize burned and its blaze crackled at the second floor of the Academic Building 1, earlier this noon of October 28, 2022. As prehistoric beasts are brought back from the dead and the fictional world converges with ours, along with many other apocalypses, one particular door had the bodies lining up. This was the most frightening faculty stall today; the Languages Unit.

Costume Day 2022 marked the week's calendar back when an email popped up in every scholar's inbox. With the exams approaching, we were told to toss aside the stress and put up the dress. However, while scholars color the halls in every hue of the rainbow, Filipino and English teachers set the mood into monochromatic.

Starting from a trick and treat in one, booming corn hurled, only being stopped by glass. These teachers appeared to be witches as they raised the seeds and bulged their flesh outwards with the twist of a hand. Afterwards, the scalding carcasses were served in paper bags, somehow expected to have been enjoyed. Sweets were also solicited as if to fatten us for baking.

Moreover, the servers brought no less shock as well. Because the devilish food wasn't enough to burn our tongues, they also brought eye candy to water our eyes. Alongside super-powered minions under the guise of children, scholars were confronted with their worst enemyâ€”their own minds. A simple representation of the fact that we're all lonely is enough to shred a teen's heart to pieces. Indeed, it was simple, but it was also cruel yet astonishingly effective at the same time.

As our lack of preparation holds us back from embracing the spookiness, the Languages Unit threw us beyond forward, almost plunging to the ground head-first. Similarly, for those who actually dressed up, we almost literally wore our hearts on our sleeves. These gave a terrifying and terrific reminder that it is in times like this, we must step back for a bit and ask ourselves the real questions. Who or what are we studying for? Where do we see ourselves in the future? Most vitally, why do I always cry whenever I see a happy couple?
